The problem

Pipelines mutate documents until nothing is reproducible.

Every AI stack invents its own intermediates: ad-hoc chunks, lossy markdown, orphan embeddings. Six months later nobody can say where a chunk came from — or rebuild it after a fix. Knowledge becomes disposable.

Questions

FAQ

Do I have to use Brain after Canonical?

No — that is the point of the fork. Canonical ends in an asset YOU own: feed your retrieval, fine-tune your models, build training data. Brain is the optional next step, not a requirement.

Is chunking a separate product?

No. Chunking is a capability of Canonical. Chunks are projections of the canonical model — you can rebuild them at any time, with proof.

Why does determinism matter?

Because trust requires replay. When a defect is found, the canonical model lets you re-derive every downstream artifact and show exactly what changed — and what did not.
