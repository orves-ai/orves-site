The problem

Chatbots answer questions. Brains accumulate knowledge.

Prompting a model over raw files produces fluent answers with invisible foundations — and knowledge bases store conclusions while discarding the evidence. The Brain keeps living, governed memory and reasons only over certified claims.

How it works

Three moves. Nothing hidden.

01

Connect

Point Brain at your sources — drives, wikis, mail, repos, databases, APIs. No manual writing, no manual organizing: it is born nearly ready.

02

Govern

Claims are resolved against evidence; contradictions surfaced; insufficient evidence means ABSTAINED, never a guess. The Observatory keeps it all current.

03

Serve

Ask anything — or connect your AI via API and MCP. Answers return with grounding, abstentions and a certificate.

Questions

FAQ

Why is this Brain different from any other knowledge system?

Because of what it stands on. Every fact in the Brain lives on a canonical, verifiable substrate: structured losslessly by Parser, made deterministic and reconstructible by Canonical, kept synchronized with reality by the Observatory. Other systems store what you tell them; this one can prove what it knows.

Does Brain compete with GPT or Claude?

No — it feeds them. Brain is where your knowledge persists; models are interfaces that connect to it. Swap the model, keep the Brain. That is the point.

What happened to the Knowledge Engine?

It is inside the Brain — the engine that governs claims, evidence and versions. You never buy it separately, the same way you never buy a database's storage engine. Brain: built on the Knowledge Engine.

Which model does the Brain use?

Any. Providers are measured and swappable, never hardcoded. Your knowledge stays independent of the model that reads it.

Is my Brain shared?

No. Each Brain instance is isolated by construction and reasons only over your governed knowledge. Nothing you ingest trains shared models.
